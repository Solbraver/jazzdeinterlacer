Why this exists

I'm an orphan of
JesDeinterlacer.

For years, JesDeinterlacer was the go-to tool for Mac editors working with interlaced footage — broadcast archives, old tapes, legacy cameras. Simple, reliable, and it just worked.

Then it stopped. No updates, no replacement, nothing. And suddenly a lot of us were left with footage we couldn't properly process on macOS without jumping through hoops or booting into Windows.

"I needed it, it didn't exist anymore, so I built it."

JazzDeinterlacer is my attempt to fill that gap — and go further. Not just a port, but a modern tool with state-of-the-art algorithms including QTGMC and neural-network optical flow, built natively for Apple Silicon.

It's still in active development. The basics are solid and production-ready. More features are coming. If you were a JesDeinterlacer user, you'll feel at home immediately.
